The tavern fire crackled as you stepped into the warmth of the Drunken Duckling. You weren’t just here for a meal; you were here to build a legacy.

Four distinct figures sat scattered across the common room, each a potential key to the Frost-Spire Mountains.

The Recruitment

You first approached a corner table where Kaelen, the paladin, sat polishing a heavy steel shield. His armor bore the dents of a dozen battles.
"The mountains are no place for the solitary," he said, his voice like grinding gravel. "My blade is sworn to protect those who seek justice. If your path is righteous, I am yours." With a firm nod, he stood, his height casting a long shadow.

Across the room, Lyra was expertly flipping a copper coin over her knuckles. The rogue caught the coin and winked as you neared.
"Kaelen’s a bit stiff, but I suppose you’ll need someone to keep him from walking into traps," she chuckled. "I’m in. But I get first dibs on any locked chests we find."

With your frontline and your scout secured, you turned to the others to prepare for the road ahead.

The Intel

Bram, the wizard, was buried under a mountain of parchment. When you asked about the Glimmer-Grot, he tapped a stained map.
"The bioluminescence isn't just for show," he whispered. "The moss grows thickest near veins of Aether-Quartz. Follow the brightest patches if it’s treasure you’re after, but keep your boots quiet. Sound carries in those crystal halls."

Finally, you spoke with Elowen, the druid, who was tending to a small potted fern by the window.
"The cave is alive," she warned softly. "You will meet the Deep-Gnomes; they are indifferent to your gold, but they value salt. If you give them a pinch from your rations, they may tell you which tunnels the Chitin-Stalkers avoid. Respect the mountain, and it might just let you leave."

The Ascent and The Cave

With Kaelen’s shield and Lyra’s daggers at your side, you climbed the shale paths until the blue glow of the Glimmer-Grot spilled out to meet you. Inside, the world was a cathedral of pulsing azure light.

Following Bram’s advice, you tracked the most vibrant moss, leading you to the central chamber. There, three Deep-Gnomes worked silently. Remembering Elowen’s words, you offered a small pouch of salt. The eldest gnome paused, his stony face softening.
"Take the left tunnel," he grunted, pocketing the gift. "The Stalkers fear the heat from the lower vents. It's the safest way to the Quartz vein."

The Shadow’s Teeth

Despite the shortcut, the darkness had ears. In the narrow "Whisper Tunnel," four Chitin-Stalkers dropped from the ceiling, their iridescent shells clicking.

Kaelen stepped forward, his shield a wall of holy light that forced the monsters back.

Lyra vanished into the shadows of the moss, reappearing behind the lead stalker to deliver a precise, distracting strike.

You led the charge, using the distraction to push through the swarm.

The Stalkers, realizing this party was far more prepared than their usual prey, skittered back into the crevices. Moments later, you emerged into a hidden grotto where the walls weren't just mossy—they were embedded with the glowing Aether-Quartz Bram had described. The mini-adventure had paid off.
